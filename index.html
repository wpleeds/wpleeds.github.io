WordPress Leeds

WordPress Leeds is an informal organisation to help promote WordPress and Blogging events in Leeds and West Yorkshire. Our main event is a monthly evening meetup for Bloggers, Journalists, Designers, Developers and anyone interested in Blogging and Digital Publishing.

Our evening event is 6:00pm on the 4th Tuesday of the month at DLA Piper. Due to space constraints the event is normally ticketed but is free!

What's on this month?

WordPress Leeds - Tuesday 24th November 2015 6pm

We have two speakers lined up for November's event: Mark Wilkinson and Matt Watson.

Mark Wilkinson - Admin Customisations for a 1 Page Website

Sometimes we build WordPress sites that are just 1 pagers. These are quite popular at the moment. We spend a lot of time, making the front end experience exiting, with features such a parallax scrolling etc. However we often neglect the admin leaving it as the default experience.
In this talk I will outline how I have removed a lot of the bloated admin interface which is not needed for a 1 page WordPress site making the editor experience much better.

About Mark

Mark is a Freelance WordPress developer working from home in Lancashire and a former secondary school teacher of Computing. He builds both large and small WordPress sites for clients and agencies across the UK and blogs about WordPress at markwilkinson.me. Mark is particularly interested in using WordPress in different ways, getting away from a CMS or blog.

Matt Watson - Getting started with WordPress and Vagrant

Many people start off developing with WordPress using MAMP, WAMP, XAMP or something similar. But they fast become unstuck when working within a team of developers.
At Make Do we use Vagrant as part of our Workflow solution, to make sure that all of our developers have access to the same environment.
I will talk about the benefits of Vagrant, some of the pitfalls, and even explore some other tools that might make your team environment a little more bearable.

About Matt

Building websites since the 90s, Matt is development lead at the WordPress Agency Make Do.

WordPress Leeds Structure & Topics

General Publishing talks

Sessions suitable for all surrounding digital publishing and blogging regardless of platform

WordPress Specific Talks

Sessions specifically talking about feature and concepts related to WordPress

Friendly Chat & Banter

Networking, rants, problem solving and general chatter in between sessions and after

Normally our monthly meetup has two speakers, one general speaker and one WordPress specific. The talks are aimed to be interesting to a broad selection of people, even if you don't use WordPress there is something for you.

Other Interesting Groups

Local WordPress Groups

WordPress Sheffield

WordPress Leeds little brother, WordPress Sheffield meets on the 2nd Tuesday of every month around Sheffield. They normally run one month social the next talks. Talks tend to be more technical, for more details see WordPress Sheffield website or follow them on Twitter @wpsheffield

WordPress York

Yorkshire newest WordPress group, which started in July 2014, they holding monthly meetings around York. For more details see the WordPress York website or follow them on Twitter @wp_york

Manchester WordPress User Group

Manchester is just an hour from Leeds on the train and home to one of the largest UK user groups run by the co-founder of WordPress Mike Little. Each month MWUG runs both a general and technical session completely devoted to WordPress. For more details see the MWUG website or follow them on Twitter @mwug

Other Leeds & Yorkshire Groups

Leeds PHP

The local PHP user group, who run monthly events around Leeds for more information see Leeds PHP site or follow them on Twitter @leedsphp

Drupal Yorkshire

It's not just WordPress that we are crazy about in Yorkshire we also have an active Drupal community who recently put on their first DrupalCamp (It's like a WordCmp but for Drupal), for more details see Drupal Yorkshire site.

Yorkshire Digital

Want to find a user group in the area, then why not subscribe to Yorkshire Digital email newsletter which each month highlights a few events going around Leeds and the Yorkshire region, to sign up visit Yorkshire Digital you can also follow them on Twitter @yorksdigital
